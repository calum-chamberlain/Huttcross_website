National Championships 2016

The Rules

These rules apply to the National Champs races being held by Huttcross on 6 August 2016. They don't apply to the Huttcross regular season races being held the following day - which are covered by the normal Huttcross race rules.

You'll be asked to agree to these rules when you enter one of these races.

Bikes

Cyclocross bikes only. That means drop handlebars and tyres no wider than 35mm. Bikes must be safe to use (including at least one functional brake). Perhaps consider consulting your local bike shop on this front.

Stuff you wear

Helmets are compulsory. Clothing must at least cover the nether regions. Shoes should be covered (no touring sandals). Race numbers must be pinned to your back.

Elite and age grade races

It's up to you whether you enter the elite (60 minute) or age grade (50 minute) races. The elite races are open to all ages. Licenses are not required for any race.

Practice

There are designated practice times in the morning and before the elite races. Between races, stay off the race course while people are still on the course racing.

The start

The race organisers have discretion to put known kick-ass racers on the front row. Otherwise it's all self seeded.

Race etiquette

Ride hard, embrace the competition, but be a decent person. No unreasonable aggression, physical violence, or mean-spirited abuse. The organisers have full discretion to pull you from the race if you misbehave.

Age grade races

These will be a combined start and finish (rather than wave starts and separate finishes for different grades). Results for each grade will be determined within the combined results.

Getting lapped

We will not be pulling riders from the race if lapped by the race leader.

Bell lap

A bell lap will run between the race leader starting their final lap and the race winner finishing. This means you may not get a bell lap if you are lapped by the leader in that time.

Finish

Return your timing chip and move away from the finish line once you have completed your race. We don't want pile ups of sprinting riders on the line. If you don't return your timing chip, you'll be charged $55 to replace it.
