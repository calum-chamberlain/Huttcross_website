Wheelworks

Thanks for the support.

Hüttcross is into its third season now and it's more popular than ever. This year we are lucky enough to have two title sponsors in Crucial Custom Cycles and Wheelworks Handcrafted Wheels. This is one of a pair of thank-you posts. First, we wish to thank Wheelworks for their support and give our fans a little information about their business and what their support means to us. Tom Lynskey, 1/6 of the Hüttcross committee, visited Wheelworks HQ to get an idea of what it was all about.

From the outset, Hüttcross sought to provide a fun and friendly event at little cost to the entrants. We were adamant that a quality series need not cost $30-$50 per event. After two and a half seasons of quality racing and emphatic turnouts, we feel we've made our point.

This year, Wheelworks approached us prior to the season to offer up a major spot prize: a set of custom wheels. Wheelworks was well known to us, as its riders are passionate racers and their presence has slowly been building from the Lyall Bay workshop. It's difficult to tell you exactly what their spot prize will look like, because it hasn't been built yet. But that's the whole point.

Wheelworks builds wheels, and that's all they do. Yet, if you rolled out to their workshop to buy some wheels for your sled, you wouldn't find a single wheel that you could purchase then and there. We can't tell you what the spot prize wheels will look like, because Wheelworks doesn't know who has won them yet.

When they build a set of wheels for a client, it begins with a conversation. The aims, dreams, quirks, proclivities, hopes and desires of the rider are all rolled into an amorphous many-limbed equation creature in the head of the Wheelworks Brains Trust. It slinks about, drawing on years of race-wrenching, product testing, tech geeking and observational experience until it breathes its own air. That unique beast is then realised - given form through rolling, cutting and drawing within the many custom tools at the lads' disposal.

The result is one-size-fits-one. The parts that go into the build are selected on the basis of quality, not distributorship loyalties. Everything is measured and cut exactly to suit - to such close detail that all the threads of the nipple are taken up, but not beyond. The whole build is then fed into an enormous hydraulic press that beds the components into place beneath a significant loading force. The benefit of this is that the wheels don't need to be ridden in, because they're already "in" when they leave the workshop. This is vital when they are sending their product out of the country.

The precision and customisation is impressive, but there is no doubt it comes at a premium price. "I'm not concerned if we lose a customer to the local bike shop down the road", Jesse explains, "unless it's because I've failed to convey what exactly we provide."

As someone who has built wheels myself, and ridden many great wheels from storied Wellington builders, I could see the case for what Wheelworks do. Aside from being a helpful resource for those riders who know what they want their wheels to do, but not how to spec to suit, Wheelworks' service should also appeal to anyone who prioritises an exacting attention to detail, and values peace of mind highly. There is something deeply and mechanically satisfying about the tolerances and processes that govern a Wheelworks build.

As far as Hüttcross is concerned, the support from Wheelworks is an affirmation for us, and a boon for the 2016 racers. Having passionate riders who actually front up to our races willing to get behind us tells us that we're on the right track. And we're very proud that we can bring a relaxed and professional event with the chance to win an outstanding spot prize that will likely serve the winner well for decades, all for a humble tenner.

Most of our riders will have a road or mountain bike, and upgrading wheels is a simple way of transforming the performance of the bike. So if you think you could benefit from a new set of wheels, we do encourage you to start your search with Wheelworks. If their service is not what you need, they'll be the first to let you know.

Failing that, you could always just make sure you win the spot prize draw on the 7th of August.
